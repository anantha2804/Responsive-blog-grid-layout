Owl

Owls are nocturnal birds of prey that belong to the Strigiformes order. They are found in different parts of the world and are known for their unique adaptations such as a silent flight, excellent hearing, and specialized feathers that allow them to fly silently. Owls have a distinct appearance with a large head, forward-facing eyes, and a hooked beak. They are known to be skilled hunters, feeding on a variety of prey such as rodents, insects, and small mammals. Some owl species are also revered in various cultures and considered symbols of wisdom and intelligence.

Parrot

Parrots are colorful birds known for their ability to mimic sounds and human speech. They belong to the Psittaciformes order and are found in different parts of the world, mostly in tropical and subtropical regions. Parrots have a strong, curved beak and zygodactyl feet (two toes pointing forward and two toes pointing backward) that allow them to climb and perch on branches. They are social birds that typically form monogamous pairs and can live for several decades in captivity. Spring Goldfinch

They are common throughout North America, and during the spring season, the males of the species develop bright yellow feathers, making them easily recognizable. In contrast, the females remain a duller yellow-brown color throughout the year. American Goldfinches are known for their distinctive and musical song and are often found in meadows, fields, and gardens.
